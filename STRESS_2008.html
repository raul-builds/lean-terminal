What the breaker does

The system runs a regime filter — hold while the trend is up, step to cash when it breaks — with one hard backstop: if the portfolio draws down 15% from its peak, everything reverts to a defensive cash posture, no discretion. That backstop fires on March 19, 2008, six months before Lehman, and keeps the book out of the worst of the collapse.

The result is not magic and it is not a prediction. It is a mechanical rule doing exactly one job: converting a portfolio-ending loss into a survivable one. Buy-and-hold ends the window down 36% having touched −60%. The system ends down 13% having touched −18%.

Honest limits

What this test does not claim

  • One episode, not a distribution. 2008 has a specific shape. 2000 and 1929 crashed differently; those replays are separate work before the tail is fully mapped.
  • Regime proxy. For a semis-only book the regime gate uses SOXX against its own 200-day average, a defensible stand-in for the live index gate but not identical to it.
  • Mostly defensive. The breaker plus bear regime kept the book in cash ~96% of the window. That is why it survived — capital preservation over participation — but it also means the system sat out most of 2008 by design.
